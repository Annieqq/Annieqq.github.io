Economic Prospects for 2018

Hong Kong Financial Secretary, Mr Paul Chan Mo-Po, forecasts GDP growth a will increase by 3 to 4 percent in 2018, mainly driven by favorable employment and income conditions spurring local consumption growth.

Sanguine local economic sentiment and positive business confidence should also sustain investment growth.

On inflation, local cost pressures will go up somewhat amid sustained economic growth.

For 2018 as a whole, Mr. Chan said that the inflation will remain moderate.



The headline inflation rate will be 2.2 percent with an underlying inflation rate at 2.5 percent .

Hong Kong Economic Situation in 2017

   Overall, Hong Kong's economy grew by 3.8 per cent last year due to the better-than-expected outturn in the external environment, higher than the forecast and the average trend growth rate of 2.9 per cent over the decade from 2007 to 2016.

   Main reasons:

  1. The global economic landscape continued to improve in 2017.

  2. The economies of the US, Eurozone and Japan recovered further with a synchronised acceleration of growth.

  3. The Mainland economy, sustaining strong growth momentum at 6.9 percent, bolstered regional trade flows in Asia.

   The headline inflation rate for 2017 was 1.5 percent, as inflationary pressure remained moderate. 

   Netting out the effects of the Government's one-off measures, the underlying inflation rate was 1.7 percent in 2017, the sixth consecutive year of easing.

Estimated Expenditure for 2018-19

   The overall expenditure of the Hong Kong government for 2018-19 is estimated to be $557.9 billion, an increase of 17.6 percent compared with the revised estimate for 2017-18.  

   Operating expenditure is estimated to be $441.5 billion, a year-on-year increase of 18.4 percent or $68.6 billion.

   Recurrent expenditure, which accounts for over 90 per cent of operating expenditure, will reach $406.5 billion, a year-on-year increase of 11.8 per cent or $42.8 billion.

   Except for the infrastructure expenditure, all other expenditure are estimated to increase in the new financial year.

Estimated Revenue for 2018-19

Estimated Revenue

   Total government revenue for 2018-19 is estimated to be $604.5 billion, of which earnings and profits tax is estimated at $218.4 billion.  The revenue from land premium is estimated to be $121 billion, while that from stamp duties is estimated to be $100 billion.
